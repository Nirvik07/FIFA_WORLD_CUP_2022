FIFA WORLD CUP

2022

The 2022 FIFA World Cup is scheduled to be the 22nd running of the FIFA World Cup competition scheduled to take place in Qatar from 20 November to 18 December 2022. This will be the first World Cup ever to be held in the Arab world, and the second World Cup held entirely in Asia!

Kick-off!

World Cup 2022 takes place in Qatar from November 20 to December 18. 32 teams to compete in eight groups. The event kicks off on Sunday November 20 at the Al Bayt Stadium when the hosts take on Ecuador in Group A.

The first two rounds of matches will kick off at 1pm, 4pm, 7pm and 10pm local time . Kick-off times for the final round of group games and knockout-round matches will be 6pm and 10pm local time . The final is scheduled to kick off at 6pm local time.

Group Draw

The 2022 World Cup will begin with 32 teams in eight groups of four:
  • Group A: Qatar, Ecuador, Senegal, Netherlands

  • Group B: England, Iran, USA, Wales

  • Group C: Argentina, Saudi Arabia, Mexico, Poland

  • Group D: France, Australia, Denmark, Tunisia

  • Group E: Spain, Costa Rica, Germany, Japan

  • Group F: Belgium, Canada, Morocco, Croatia

  • Group G: Brazil, Serbia, Switzerland, Cameroon

  • Group H: Portugal, Ghana, Uruguay, South Korea

Match Format

Four matches will be played each day during the group stage, which will run over a 12-day period and see winners and runners-up progress to the round of 16. There will be a third-place play-off game on December 17.

Venues

The group games will take place across eight stadia: Al Bayt Stadium, Khalifa International Stadium, Al Thumama Stadium, Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium, Lusail Stadium, Ras Abu Aboud Stadium, Education City Stadium, Al Janoub Stadium.
